Andaman and Nicobar Islands

ANDAMAN AND NICOBAR ISLANDS

Andaman and Nicobar Islands, also popular as Kalapani is one of the most beautiful places to visit in India. Consisting of 572 Islands, this place is the perfect getaway for tourists who love to explore the marine world. Out of these 572 Islands, 50 are inhabited by the people where the rest of the Islands are either not that large for inhabitants, come under the forest reserve or it is used by the military of India as their base.

The best time to visit Andaman & Nicobar Islands is considered between October and May as the weather is pleasant, just perfect for sightseeing, water sports, and beach excursion. Monsoons (July to September) are not always recommended because of the high tidal waves, incessant rains and strong winds.

So, sooth your senses in the refreshing land of ANDAMAN AND NICOBAR ISLANDS!

1. Radhanagar Beach

  • About:

    • Awarded with the title of 'Asia's Best Beach' by the TIMES magazine in 2004, Radhanagar Beach in Havelock Island with its turquoise blue waters and powdery white sands lives up to the moniker. Its vast shores of endless white grains stretch across 2 kilometres, making it a prime attraction of the Andaman and Nicobar Islands.You can also book beach beds, parasols, and umbrellas while they spend hours merely lounging at this exquisite wonderland. Radhanagar Beach Andaman is just adjacent to Elephant beach, so you may find mahouts taking out their elephants for a joyride to the tourists.

  • Timings:

    • 6:00 AM - 5:00 PM(Everyday)

  • Location:

    • Havelock Island, South Andaman District,Andaman

  • Entry Fee:

    • None

2. Cellular Jail

  • Brief History:

    • The Cellular Jail, also known as 'Kala Pani' is an old colonial prison situated in Port Blair, the capital of Andaman and Nicobar islands. Constructed by the Britishers during their colonial rule in India, the jail was used particularly to exile Indian political prisoners. It was used to house many notable freedom fighters such as Batukeshwar Dutt, Yogendra Shukla and Vinayak Damodar Savarkar.

  • About:

    • The jail complex is now owned by the Government of India and it is recognised as the national memorial monument that showcases the life of prisoners during the British era. Cellular Jail stands as a prominent tourist attraction in the Andaman and Nicobar Islands where visitors can learn about its history and also enjoy a light show held in memory of all the prisoners who were imprisoned here.

  • Timings:

    • 9:00 AM to 12:30 PM & 1:30 PM to 5:00 PM (Closed on Mondays and public holidays)

  • Location:

    • Lecture Hall 2, GB Pant Exit Gate, Atlanta Point, Port Blair, Andaman and Nicobar Islands 744104

  • Entry Fee:

    • INR 30 for Indian Nationals
    INR 100 for Foreign Nationals

3. Ross Island

  • About:

    • Located just 2 kilometres east of Port Blair is Ross Island (now known as Netaji Subhash Bose Island); an island which was once the administrative headquarters of the British, but today is only an uninhabited island. Following an earthquake that hit Ross Island in the year 1941, the British left the island and shifted their settlement to Port Blair.Today, everything has disappeared except a few buildings, which housed some of these landmarks.

  • Timings:

    • 8:00 AM - 6:00 PM

  • Location:

    • Ross Island, Near Port Blair,Andaman and Nicobar Islands

  • Entry Fee:

    • INR 20 per person

4. Mount Harriet National Park

  • About:

    • Off the nine national parks housed by the Andaman island, Mount Harriet National Park is undoubtedly the most beautiful one. Located in Ferrargunj Tehsil of Port Blair, Mount Harriet is the third highest peak (365m) of Andaman and Nicobar and the most easily accessible one as well.Due to its privileged elevated location, the park offers some astounding views of azure blues surrounding the island along with fascinating sunset and sunrise experience as well.

  • Timings:

    • Monday to Sunday – 7.00 AM to 5.00 PM

  • Location:

    • Hope Town, Port Blair, Andaman and Nicobar Islands 744107

  • Entry Fee:

    • Adults (Indian) – INR 25 per person per day
    Students (Indian) – INR 5 per person per day
    Adults (Foreigners) – INR 250 per person per day
    Students (Foreigners) – INR 10 per person per day

5. Elephant Beach

  • About:

    • With its brilliant coral reefs and alluring cyan waters, the Elephant beach is one of the locations that add to the beach paradise reputation of the Andaman and Nicobar Islands. These waters are a prime destination for tourists wishing to engage in water sports like snorkelling, swimming, banana boat rides, speed boats and diving.One could also engage in other adventurous activities like bird-watching, trekking and kayaking. As this area is famous for its coral reefs, sea walking is a major activity undertaken by almost every visitor to this beach, making this one of the best beaches of India.

  • Timings:

    • 9:30 AM - 4:00 PM(Everyday)

  • Location:

    • SH4, Havelock Island, Port Blair, Andaman, Andaman and Nicobar Islands, 744211

  • Entry Fee:

    • None

6. Baratang Island

  • About:

    • The island is well-known for its mangroves, mud volcanoes (only ones in India) and limestone caves. However, you may need to take permission from the Forest Department at Baratang to explore these caves.Unlike Havelock Island, Baratang Island is not as developed for urban tourism and mostly serves as a hub for natural sightseeing. You can reach Baratang island via the government ferry service that runs between Port Blair city at the Phoenix Bay Jetty.

  • Timings:

    • Open all the year round

  • Location:

    • Baratang Island, Port Blair, Andaman and Nicobar Islands

  • Entry Fee:

    • None

7. Mahatma Gandhi Marine National Park

  • About:

    • Covering an area of 280 sq km with 15 islands of mangrove creeks, tropical rainforest and reefs supporting 50 types of coral, Mahatma Gandhi Marine National Park is the go-to place for snorkeling, glass bottom boat rides and scuba diving. The National Park consists of 15 islands and covers an area of 281.5 km². Being an ecotourism spot, you’ll find this place at its most pristine form and is one of the best tourist places in Andaman.

  • Timings:

    • Open all the year round

  • Location:

    • SH8, Andaman and Nicobar Islands

  • Entry Fee:

    • INR 50(Adults) per person
