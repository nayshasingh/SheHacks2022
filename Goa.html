1. Aguada Fort

  • Brief History:

    • The construction of the fort Aguada started in the 1609 CE and it was inaugurated in the year 1612 in order to keep vigil over the waters from foreign intrusions. This fort signifies the glory of the Portuguese settlement in Indian sub continent.Other than the light house Aguada Jail, the St Lawrence Church etc are good spots to visit.

  • About:

    • This place is a great tourist magnet because of its multi-facial attraction. The history of the fort that glorifies the Portuguese empire and its connections with India interests history buffs while others ate mesmerised by the sheer robustness of the fort and the vista it presents of the sea and the sun.

  • Timings:

    • 9:00 AM-6:00 PM(Everyday)

  • Location:

    • Fort Aguada Rd, Aguada Fort Area, Candolim, Goa 403515

  • Entry Fee:

    • INR 25(Indian Nationals)
    INR 300(Foreign Nationals)

3. Chapora Fort

  • Brief History:

    • The village of Chapora, and the fort, get their name from ‘Shahapura’ or, ‘the town of the Shah’. This was due to the fact that it was once the stronghold of the Sultanate of Bijapur. When the Portuguese defeated the Shah and seized the fort, it had great military significance for them, as the Chapora river marked the northern boundary of Goa, with Pernem on it’s opposite bank, being the province of the Maharaja of Sawantwadi.

  • About:

    • There was once a church dedicated to St. Anthony that stood within the walls, however it is no longer standing. When the Portuguese rebuilt the fort in 1717, they added tunnels to allow to the fort’s defenders a safe retreat should their battlements be breached, a fortuitous decision as it turned out.

  • Timings:

    • 7:00 AM - 7:00 PM(Everyday)

  • Location:

    • Chapora Fort Trail, Vagator, Goa 403509

  • Entry Fee:

    • None

5. Bom Jesus Basilica

  • Brief History:

    • The Basilica of Bom Jesus Church located in Goa is one of a kind in India and is known for its exemplary baroque architecture. Built in 1594 and consecrated in 1605, the building of this church coincides with the beginning of Christianity in India.The Basilica of Bom Jesus has been declared a World Heritage Site by UNESCO.

  • About:

    • The Basilica of Bom Jesus is over 408 years old and is open to the public every day.Literally translating to 'Holy Jesus', this is the only church in Old Goa that is not been plastered on the outside. The facade of the church has triangular roofing which is delicately carved with the initials 'IHS', meaning 'Jesus, Saviour of Men'.

  • Timings:

    • Monday - Saturday: 09:00 AM - 06:30 PM Sunday: 10:30 AM - 06:30 PM

  • Location:

    • Old Goa Road, Bainguinim, Goa 403402

  • Entry Fee:

    • None

6. Chorao Island

  • About:

    • Chorao Island is an island along the river Mandovi near Panaji. The Chorao Island was called Chudamani meaning stunning precious stone in Sanskrit. Legend says that the island emerged from the diamonds thrown away by Yashoda, Lord Krishna's mother. Chorao Island is the largest island among other 17 islands of Goa.It is full of dense mangrove swamps and the best way to navigate is by a canoe. The Sanctuary is home to a variety of birds, animals and quite a few muggers the Goan crocodiles.

  • Timings:

    • Open all the year round

  • Location:

    • Near Panaji, Goa

  • Entry Fee:

    • None

7. Se Cathedral

  • Brief History:

    • It is also known as St. Catherine's' Cathedral. It was on the feast day of St. Catherine of Alexandria when in the year of 1510, Alfonso Albuquerque overthrew the Islamic Army and captured Goa.Se cathedral is also known to be one of the oldest and the most revered religious place in Goa. It has been recognized by the UNESCO as a heritage site as well.

  • About:

    • Se cathedral stands beautifully with its marvelous architecture. It has Portuguese styled architecture. Se cathedral also has five massive bells. One of the largest bells in Goa, 'Golden Bell' is also placed here. You will also find various old painting, depicting the life of St. Catherine of Alexandria at the altar.

  • Timings:

    • 7:30 AM - 6:00 PM (Everyday)

  • Location:

    • Velha, Goa 403402

  • Entry Fee:

    • None
