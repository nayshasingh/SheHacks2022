5. GOLCONDA FORT

Golconda Fort is located in the western part of the city of Hyderabad and is located 9 km from Hussain Sagar Lake. The outer tower occupies an area of ​​three square miles, which is 4.8 miles long. Originally known as Mankal, it was built on a mountain in the year 1143.
Originally it was a mud fort under the rule of Rajah of Warangal. It was later established between the 14th and 17th centuries by the Bahmani Sultan and was followed by the ruling dynasty of Qutub Shahi. Golconda was the capital of the Qutub Shahi dynasty. The inner fort consists of the ruins of palaces, mosques and a hill top pavilion, which rises to about 130 feet [130 m] and provides a bird's view of other buildings.

  • Location: Hyderabad,Telangana
