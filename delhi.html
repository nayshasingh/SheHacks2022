Delhi

Delhi is a symbol of the country’s rich past and thriving present, the city where ancient and modern blend seamlessly together. There is a lot to explore in this city, be it its heritage, art and crafts, diverse cuisine and culture.

The best time to visit Delhi is from October to March as weather is at its best. During this period flowers are blooming, and it becomes an enjoyable experience to explore the city.

1.INDIA GATE:



  • HISTORICAL MONUMENT

  • About:

    • Original name of India Gate is All India War Memorial .It was built in order to commemorate the 82000 soldiers of the Indian Army who lost their lives during the First World War -1914 to 1921 and in the Third Anglo-Afghan War.

  • Open: 24 hours

  • Entry fee: none

  • location: Rajpath Marg, India Gate, New Delhi,110001.

2.AKSHARDHAM TEMPLE



  • HISTORICAL MONUMENT

  • About:

    • It is a spiritual and cultural campus in Delhi and was opened in 2005 by Dr APJ Abdul Kalam. It has an Abhishek Mandap, a theme-based garden, Sahaj Anand Water Show and three exhibitions like Neelkanth Darshan , Sahajanand Darshan, and Sanskruthi Darshan- a cultural boat ride.

  • Open:09:30 am to 06:30 pm, closed on Monday

  • Entry fee:Rs 170 (Adults); Rs 100 (Children)

  • location:NH 24, Akshardham Setu, New Delhi, 110092.

3.RED FORT



  • HISTORICAL MONUMENT

  • About:

    • historical fort in Delhi a brilliant architectural beauty.It can be considered as one of the best place to visit in Old Delhi as it is also known to be a symbol of India's struggle for freedom. It hosts the Independence Day celebrations every year.

  • Open:The Fort is closed on Monday, open on all other days from 09:30 am - 04:30 pm.

  • Entry fee:Indian citizens- Rs.10, for foreigners-Rs.150.

  • location:Netaji Subhash Marg, Lal Qila, Chandni Chowk, New Delhi, Delhi 110006

4.JANTAR MANTAR



  • HISTORICAL MONUMENT

  • About:

    • Jantar Mantar in Delhi is among the five major Jantar Mantar constructed by Maharaja Jai Singh II of Jaipur, has got equal recognition as the Jaipur. At Jantar Mantar, there are 13 architectural astronomy instruments. It was designed in order to compile the astronomical tables and predict the movement and timings of the sun, moon and planets.

  • Open:on all days from 09:00 am to 07:00 pm.

  • Entry fee:Indian citizens-INR 5, foreign nationals- INR 100

  • location:Sansad Marg, Connaught Place, New Delhi, Delhi 110001.

5.CHANDNI CHOWK



  • MARKET

  • About:

    • The Chandni Chowk is also called by the name of Moonlight square and it is one of the oldest but busiest markets in Delhi. It is a major attraction in India as this vibrant market provides all the items from A to Z at the best rates. There are separate markets for sarees, food, electronic items, accessories, suits ornaments, footwear, shawls, sherwanis, decoration materials, dresses etc.

  • Open:on all days except on Monday from 10:00 am - 07:00 pm.

  • Entry fee:none

  • location:Kaccha Bagh Area, Old Delhi, New Delhi,110006.

6.RASHTRAPATI BHAVAN



  • PRESIDENTIAL PALACE

  • About:

    • The Rashtrapati Bhavan is the official residence of President of India. It is a huge presidential palace and is the largest home of any head of state in the world.It has a marvelous architecture. The entire 130 hectare President Estate includes huge gardens known as the Mughal Gardens, large open spaces, a residence of office staff and bodyguards, stables etc as a major portion of the mansion.

  • Open:: 09:00 am to 04:00 pm o Circuit 1: Open on Thursday-Sunday o Circuit 2: Open on all days except Monday o Circuit 3: Open on Thursday-Sunday (November-March

  • Entry fee:Rs.50

  • location:Rashtrapati Bhawan, President's Estate, New Delhi,110004

7.JAMA MASJID



  • MOSQUE

  • About:

    • It is one of the largest mosques in India which was built by Mughal Emperor Shah Jahan between 1644 and 1656. Imam from Bukhara inaugurated the building after its completion. The mosque is protected by three great gates and four towers. Also, there are two 40 meter high minarets created on red sandstone and white marble standing. The masjid that got its life because of the architectural brilliance of Ustad Khalil is now protected by Delhi Waqf Board.

  • Open:all days from 07:00 am to 12:00 pm and from 1:30 pm to 06:30 pm.

  • Entry fee:none

  • location:Meena Bazaar, Jama Masjid, Chandni Chowk, New Delhi, Delhi 110006

8.AGRASEN KI BAOLI



  • HISTORICAL MONUMENT-stepwell

  • About:

    • A protected monument located near Connaught Place. This is a deep stepwell that has 108 steps and is said to have been built by the Agrawal community in the 14th century. You can spend up to an hour here marvelling the architectural brilliance. Shooting of the super hit Bollywood film PK was done here.

  • Open:all days of the week from 07:30 am to 06:00 pm.

  • Entry fee:none

  • location:Hailey Road, Near KG Marg, Connaught Place, New Delhi 110001, India

9.QUTUB MINAR



  • HISTORICAL MONUMENT

  • About:

    • A valuable, protected structure of India is Qutub Minar. Qutub Minar is a 73-metre tall minaret, named after Qutb-ud-din Aibak. The tower contains 5 distinct tapering storeys, wherein first 3 storeys of this masterpiece are adorned with red sandstone, while the fourth and fifth storeys are constructed in marble and sandstone respectively, engraved with decorative Quranic texts.

  • Open:on all days from 7:00 am to 5:00 pm

  • Entry fee:INR 30 (Indians); INR 500 (foreign nationals)

  • location:Qutub Minar Delhi is situated in Mehrauli in South West district of New Delhi, India.

10.LOTUS TEMPLE



  • Bahá’í House of Worship

  • About:

    • This temple is dedicated to harmony of humanity. Inspired by the lotus flower, the temple boasts of 27 free-standing marble-clad petals, and is surrounded by sprawling gardens and pond. With a height of over 34 metres, it can accommodate around 2500 people altogether. The temple welcomes every religion symbolizing the culture of Bahá’í Houses of Worship, and offers a tranquil ambience ideal for rejuvenation.

  • Open:During summer, the entry timing is from 09:00 am to 07:00 pm while in winter it is from 09:00 am to 05:30 pm on all days except Mondays

  • Entry fee:none

  • location:Lotus Temple Rd, Bahapur, Shambhu Dayal Bagh, Kalkaji, New Delhi, Delhi 110019.
