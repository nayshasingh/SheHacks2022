One of the seven wonders of the world, the Taj Mahal is located on the banks of the Yamuna River in Agra. It was built by Mughal Emperor Shah Jahan in memory of his third wife, Mumtaz Mahal. It also has the tomb of Shah Jahan himself. Built entirely of white marble in the 17th century, the Taj Mahal is one of the finest monuments in the world. FATEHPUR SIKRI

Located about 40 kilometers from Agra, Fatehpur Sikri is a town in the region of Agra and a tourist attraction. A city strongly constructed of red sandstone, Fatehpur Sikri was founded in 1571 by Mughal Emperor Akbar. In fact, the fortified city was built by the king and was the capital of his kingdom for fifteen years. It is now a UNESCO World Heritage Site, home to Jodha Bai Palace, Jama Mosque, Buland Darwaza, and Tomb of Salim Chisti among many other famous monuments. Taken from Arab and Central Asian tent furniture, the Imperial Complex is a work of formal geometric pairs in a piece of land.

BARA IMAMBARA

Built by Nawab Asaf-ud-Daula between 1785 and 1791 AD, the main purpose of the building was to provide jobs. There are a few fascinating legends associated with this monument, which is undoubtedly one of the most fascinating sites in Lucknow, Uttar Pradesh. According to one of them, the common people worked during the day to build its foundation, while the nobles and other members of the special assembly demolished whatever was built at night! The purpose was to prolong the work as long as possible, or at least, as long as the famine continued.

      • DASHASHWAMEDHA GHAT,VARANASI

      The Varanasi ghats have served, and continue to serve, for a variety of purposes, especially spiritual ones. One of those is the Dashashwamedh Ghat, one of the oldest and most famous ghats in Varanasi. Not to be overlooked is the magnificent Vishwanath Temple, which attracts devotees and visitors alike.
      There are two mythological myths surrounding its name. One of them claims to have been created by King Brahma to receive King Shiva, while another claims that King Brahma donated (medh) 10 (das) horses (aswa) during the time of Ashwamedha Yajna. According to historical sources, the ghat was built by Bajirao Pesha I in the year 1740 AD before it was rebuilt by Ahilyabai Holkar, Queen of Indore, in 1774 AD.

      AGRA FORT,AGRA


      Agra Fort, as we know it today, is a magnificent masterpiece made by the most famous Mughal emperor, Akbar in 1565 AD, succeeded by Shah Jahan. Prior to Akbar, the fort had functioned as the abode of Babur, while Humayun was crowned emperor in it. It is one of the three UNESCO World Heritage Sites in Agra, with the exception of the Taj Mahal and Fatehpur Sikri. This beautiful monument is also known as Lal Qila and Qila-I-Akbari, because it is made of red sandstone. Inside the fort, you encounter a few architecture that combines perfection of beauty and elegance. No treasure trip to the UP is completed without a visit to Agra Fort.


      ADDRESS:-Agra Fort, Rakabganj, Agra, Uttar Pradesh 282003


      HOW TO REACH


      Agra Fort is well connected by road from nearby cities such as Delhi, Jaipur and Lucknow. The distance from Agra Fort from Delhi is approximately 230 km from the Yamuna Expressway - a six-lane highway that can be reached to Agra from Delhi in about three hours.


      The nearest airport to Agra Fort


      Agra Airport is the Indian Air Force airport and serves the public. Known as Agra Civil or Kheria, the airport is located 5 km from the city.


      The nearest railway station to Agra Fort


      The Agra Fort railway station located in Mantola is the nearest railway station. It is about 2 km from Agra Fort. Alternatively, you can take the train to Agra Cantonment which is the largest railway station in Agra and 5 km from Agra Fort.
